Counseling Psychology FAQ

- Counseling psychology is a specialty within psychology that focuses on facilitating personal and interpersonal functioning across the lifespan. It addresses emotional, social, work, school, and physical health concerns through various therapeutic techniques.

- Counseling psychology can help with a wide range of issues including anxiety, depression, relationship problems, stress management, career counseling, grief and loss, and personal development.

- In your first session, Dr. Niyanta will typically gather information about your background, current issues, and goals for therapy. It’s also an opportunity to discuss confidentiality, therapy approaches, and establish a rapport.

- Most counseling sessions last between 45 to 60 minutes. The length and frequency of sessions depend on your individual needs and the therapist’s approach.

- The number of sessions varies based on the nature of your concerns and your progress. Some issues may require short-term therapy, while others might benefit from a longer-term approach. Keeping in mind these requirements we have tailor- made packages for you to choose from.

- Yes, counseling is confidential. We adhere to strict ethical guidelines to protect your privacy. Exceptions include situations involving harm to yourself or others, abuse, or court orders.

- It’s important to feel comfortable with your counselor. If you feel uneasy, discuss your concerns with them. If the issue persists, consider seeking a different counselor who may be a better fit.

- Consider counseling if you’re experiencing persistent emotional distress, difficulty managing stress, relationship problems, or a desire for personal growth. A consultation session with Dr. Niyanta can help determine if counseling is appropriate for you.

- Yes, counseling is effective for treating anxiety and depression. Therapists use various evidence-based techniques such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) to help manage and reduce symptoms.

- The terms are often used interchangeably, but counseling generally focuses on specific issues and is shorter-term, while therapy tends to be longer-term and addresses broader patterns of behavior and emotional issues.

- Yes, we offer online sessions also known as teletherapy, which can provide flexibility and convenience for those unable to attend in-person sessions.

-Yes we take couple counselling sessions for enhancing communication, resolving conflicts, and strengthening emotional connections. It provides tools to rebuild trust, improve intimacy, and navigate life changes, fostering a healthier and more fulfilling relationship.

- Counseling can help you identify the sources of stress, develop coping strategies, and implement techniques such as relaxation exercises, time management, and cognitive restructuring to manage stress more effectively.

- Yes, counseling can be very beneficial for children and adolescents, addressing issues such as behavioral problems, anxiety, depression, family conflicts, and developmental concerns.

- Most of our sessions are done online but in case you wish to visit the clinic in person, we have kept 5 slots for Sundays that you can book on special request.

- Group counseling provides support from peers facing similar issues, offers different perspectives, enhances social skills, and helps individuals realize they are not alone in their struggles.

- Reflect on the issues you want to address, be open to discussing your feelings and experiences, and consider setting specific goals for what you hope to achieve through counseling.

- It’s okay if you’re unsure what to talk about. Dr. Niyanta will guide the session, ask questions, and help you explore your thoughts and feelings. Over time, topics will naturally arise.

- Signs that counseling is working include feeling heard and understood, experiencing emotional relief, gaining new insights, and noticing positive changes in your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.
